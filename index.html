Rules of Engagement

01

Capture the Flag

Each challenge hides a secret flag formatted as FLAG{...}. Find it, submit it, claim your points. Flags are case-sensitive — precision matters.

02

Solo Investigation

Each agent competes independently. Sharing flags or solutions is strictly prohibited. Collaboration outside the sanctioned platform will result in disqualification.

03

No Sabotage

Do not attempt to disrupt the platform, attack infrastructure, or interfere with other agents' sessions. The only target is the challenge itself.

04

Time is Evidence

The competition runs for a fixed window. Faster solves earn bonus points. The clock is always ticking — manage your time like a seasoned detective.

05

Point Scoring

Challenges are weighted by difficulty: Easy (100 pts), Medium (250 pts), Hard (500 pts). First blood earns a 50-point bonus. Final scores determine the leaderboard.

06

Fair Play

Automated brute-force, flag guessing scripts, or any automated exploit tools are forbidden. Use your mind, not your machines — this is a test of skill.

What is CTF?

Capture The Flag (CTF) is a cybersecurity competition where participants solve security challenges to find hidden strings called flags. Each flag proves you've cracked the puzzle.

Challenges span cryptography, digital forensics, steganography, web hacking, reverse engineering, and more. CTFs are used worldwide to train the next generation of security professionals.

EduCTF 2026 is organized by the PUP Sto. Tomas Campus Computer Society to cultivate digital defense skills among students. Whether you're a rookie detective or a seasoned hacker — every agent is welcome.
